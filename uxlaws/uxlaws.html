🧠 UX Laws & Design Psychology

Hick’s Law
More options lead to harder decisions. Reduce choices to improve speed.
💼 Confirmation Bias
People seek info that supports their beliefs. Use this to reinforce positive actions.
👁 Priming
What users see first shapes how they behave or feel later.
🚛 Cognitive Load
The less users have to think, the smoother their journey. Simplify tasks.
⚓️ Anchoring Bias
Users rely heavily on first impressions — make that count!
👉 Nudge
Subtle design nudges can guide users toward beneficial decisions.
🍰 Progressive Disclosure
Show users only what they need now — reveal more as needed.
